What's a Blue Cheese Crusted Filet Mignon with Port Wine Sauce

Indulge in the epitome of luxurious dining with the Blue Cheese Crusted Filet Mignon with Port Wine Sauce. This exquisite dish combines the tender perfection of filet mignon with the bold and savory notes of blue cheese, creating a culinary symphony that delights the senses. The filet mignon, cooked to a precise medium-rare, boasts a mouthwatering tenderness that yields effortlessly to the touch of a knife. Encased in a flavorful blue cheese crust, the steak takes on a new dimension, marrying the richness of the meat with the piquancy of the cheese. As a crowning glory, a velvety port wine sauce cascades over the steak, infusing every bite with a symphony of deep, complex flavors. This gastronomic masterpiece is a celebration of the finest ingredients and culinary craftsmanship.

Recipe

Crafting the Blue Cheese Crusted Filet Mignon with Port Wine Sauce demands meticulous attention to detail. Begin by selecting prime filet mignon cuts, ensuring they are trimmed to perfection. The steaks are then generously coated with a harmonious blend of blue cheese, breadcrumbs, and select herbs, creating a crust that will enhance the steak's natural succulence. The filet mignon is seared to a golden perfection, locking in the juices and creating a tantalizing contrast with the crispy blue cheese exterior. While the steaks rest, the chef prepares a velvety port wine sauce, reducing the wine to a luscious consistency that will complement the richness of the steak. Plated with artful precision, the Blue Cheese Crusted Filet Mignon is served with a generous drizzle of the port wine sauce, creating a culinary masterpiece that elevates the classic filet mignon to unparalleled heights of sophistication and flavor. Each forkful is a symphony of textures and tastes, making this dish a true indulgence for the connoisseur's palate.

Ingredients

    1 tablespoon butter
    ½ cup minced white onion
    3 cloves garlic, minced
    1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
    ¾ cup low-sodium beef broth
    ½ cup port wine
    1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    4 filet mignon steaks (1 1/2 inch thick)
    ¾ cup crumbled blue cheese
    ¼ cup panko bread crumbs

Directions

    1 Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic and thyme. Cook, stirring constantly, until onion is tender. Stir in the beef broth, scraping any onion bits from the bottom of the pan, then stir in the port wine. Bring to a boil, and cook until the mixture has reduced to about 1/2 cup. Set aside. This may also be made ahead of time, and reheated.
    2 Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Heat oil in a cast-iron or other oven-safe skillet over high heat. Sear steaks quickly on both sides until brown, then place the whole pan into the oven.
    3 Roast steaks in the oven for about 15 minutes for medium rare - with an internal temperature of 145 degrees F (63 degrees C). You may adjust this time to allow the steaks to finish just below your desired degree of doneness if medium is not what you prefer. Remove from the oven, and place on a baking sheet. Stir together the panko crumbs and blue cheese. Top each steak with a layer of this mixture.
    4 Preheat the oven's broiler. Place steaks under the preheated broiler until the cheese topping is browned and bubbly. 3 to 4 minutes. Remove from the oven, and let stand for at least 15 minutes before serving. Serve with warm port wine sauce.
