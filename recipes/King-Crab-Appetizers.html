What's a King Crab Appetizers

The King Crab Cutlet is a culinary masterpiece that stands as a testament to the opulence of seafood cuisine. This regal dish features a succulent and sizable portion of king crab meat, renowned for its sweet and delicate flavor. The crab cutlet is expertly prepared, showcasing a golden-brown exterior that hints at the perfect balance between crispiness and tenderness within. With each indulgent bite, diners are treated to a burst of oceanic richness, accentuated by the natural sweetness of the king crab. Complemented by a light drizzle of lemon-infused butter sauce, the King Crab Cutlet elevates the dining experience, offering a harmonious marriage of premium ingredients and culinary finesse.

Recipe

The creation of the King Crab Cutlet is a meticulous dance of culinary artistry, starting with the selection of the finest king crab legs. The chef carefully extracts the succulent crab meat, ensuring each luscious strand is free from any impurities. To enhance the natural sweetness and flavor, a light seasoning is applied, allowing the essence of the king crab to shine. The delicate crab meat is then expertly formed into sizable cutlets, each one a testament to the chef's skill and precision. The preparation continues with a masterful coating process, where the crab cutlets are enveloped in a seasoned batter that guarantees a perfect balance of crispiness upon frying. The cutlets take a luxurious bath in bubbling golden oil, transforming into a visual and gustatory delight. The final touch involves a decadent lemon-infused butter sauce, drizzled artfully over the golden cutlets. This buttery elixir not only complements the sweet crab meat but also adds a touch of citrus brightness, creating a symphony of flavors that dance on the palate. The result is the King Crab Cutlet—a culinary masterpiece that captures the essence of indulgence and sophistication.

Ingredients

    2 tablespoons thinly sliced green onion
    1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
    1 (6 ounce) can crab meat, drained
    2 tablespoons mayonnaise
    2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
    ½ cup shredded Cheddar cheese
    2 (12 ounce) packages refrigerated biscuit dough
    1 (8 ounce) package cream cheese, softened
    1 pinch paprika

steps

    1 Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Lightly grease 12 tartlet pans.
    2 Divide rolls in half and press into the prepared tartlet pans. Set aside.
    3 In a large bowl, combine cream cheese, crab, mayonnaise, Parmesan cheese, Cheddar cheese, green onions and Worcestershire sauce. Spoon 1 teaspoon of mixture into tarts and garnish with paprika.
    4 Bake at 375 degrees F (190 degrees C) for 15 to 20 minutes, or until light brown. These freeze wonderfully. Just reheat before serving
